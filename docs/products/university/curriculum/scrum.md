1. What are Tuckman's stages of team development? — **Answer:** Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning
2. What is psychological safety and why does it matter? — **Answer:** The belief that you can speak up, take risks, and make mistakes without punishment — it's the #1 predictor of team effectiveness

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is active listening? — **Answer:** Fully focusing on the speaker, reflecting back what you hear, asking clarifying questions, and withholding judgment
2. What makes a meeting effective? — **Answer:** Clear purpose, right attendees, agenda, time management, action items with owners, and follow-up

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. Why is scaling Agile difficult? — **Answer:** More teams mean more dependencies, communication overhead, coordination complexity, and risk of losing Agile values
2. What is SAFe? — **Answer:** Scaled Agile Framework — a comprehensive framework for scaling Agile across large enterprises with multiple teams, programs, and portfolios
3. What is LeSS? — **Answer:** Large-Scale Scrum — a minimalist framework that scales Scrum by keeping it simple and applying Scrum principles at scale

5. What is the most important principle when scaling Agile? — **Answer:** Decentralize decision-making — push decisions to the teams closest to the work

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What are the core practices of Kanban? — **Answer:** Visualize work, limit WIP, manage flow, make policies explicit, implement feedback loops, and improve collaboratively
2. What is a WIP limit? — **Answer:** Work In Progress limit — the maximum number of items allowed in a column at any time, preventing overload and exposing bottlenecks
3. What is cycle time? — **Answer:** The time from when work starts on an item to when it's completed — a key flow metric
4. What is the difference between Kanban and Scrum? — **Answer:** Kanban is continuous flow with no fixed iterations; Scrum uses fixed-length sprints with defined roles and events
5. How do you identify a bottleneck in a Kanban system? — **Answer:** Look for the column with the most accumulated work — that's where flow is constrained

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is a value stream? — **Answer:** The end-to-end sequence of activities required to deliver value to a customer — from idea to production
2. What is flow efficiency? — **Answer:** The ratio of active work time to total lead time — most organizations have flow efficiency below 15%
3. What is the difference between lead time and cycle time? — **Answer:** Lead time is from request to delivery; cycle time is from work start to delivery
4. How do you identify waste in a value stream? — **Answer:** Map every step, categorize as value-adding or non-value-adding, and look for delays, rework, handoffs, and waiting
5. What is the Theory of Constraints? — **Answer:** A management philosophy that identifies the most limiting factor (constraint) and systematically improves it until it's no longer the bottleneck
