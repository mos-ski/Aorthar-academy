## YEAR 100 — AGILE FOUNDATIONS

**Goal:** Produce graduates who are experts in process, efficiency, and team dynamics. Combines Scrum Mastery, Project Management, and Product Operations.

### Semester 1

**SCR101 — Agile Principles & Scrum Mastery** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* The entry point for aspiring Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches — history of Agile, Scrum framework, and servant-leadership mindset.
*Classes:*
1. Waterfall vs Agile — History of software development, limitations of Waterfall
2. The Agile Manifesto — 4 Values and 12 Principles
3. Scrum Framework Overview — Empiricism, Scrum Values
4. Scrum Roles: The Scrum Master — Servant leadership, removing impediments
5. Scrum Roles: PO & Developers — Product Owner vs Developer accountability
6. Scrum Artifacts: The Backlog — Product vs Sprint Backlog, Definition of Done
7. Scrum Artifacts: The Increment — Shippable increment, technical debt
8. Scrum Events: Sprint Planning — Estimation, Story Points, Sprint Goal
9. Scrum Events: Daily Scrum — Purpose, avoiding status updates, 3 questions
10. Scrum Events: Sprint Review — Inspecting increment, stakeholder feedback
11. Scrum Events: Sprint Retrospective — Continuous improvement, facilitation techniques
12. Kanban & Scrumban — Visualizing work, WIP limits, flow efficiency

**PM101 — Foundations of Product Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Core PM concepts — product lifecycle, user needs, strategy, and roadmapping.

**PSY101 — Team Dynamics & Psychology** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand team psychology — group dynamics, motivation, conflict, and building high-performing teams.

**COM101 — Communication & Facilitation** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Master communication skills — active listening, facilitation techniques, and stakeholder communication.

**BUS101 — Business Fundamentals for Tech** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Business basics — revenue models, unit economics, market analysis, and business strategy.

### Semester 2

**SCR102 — Advanced Scrum & Facilitation** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Advanced Scrum practices — large-scale Scrum, facilitation patterns, and coaching teams through challenges.

**OPS101 — Introduction to Product Operations** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Product operations fundamentals — tooling, processes, data management, and operational excellence.

**PM102 — Writing User Stories & Requirements** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Write clear, actionable user stories and acceptance criteria for development teams.

**ANA101 — Data Literacy & Reporting** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Read and interpret data — metrics, dashboards, reporting, and data-driven decision making.

**LDR101 — Introduction to Leadership** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Leadership fundamentals — self-awareness, influence, decision-making, and leading without authority.
