*Quiz (5 questions):*

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is a Work Breakdown Structure? — **Answer:** A hierarchical decomposition of the total scope of work — breaking the project into manageable components
2. What is a Gantt chart? — **Answer:** A bar chart that shows the project schedule — tasks, durations, dependencies, and progress over time
3. What is the critical path? — **Answer:** The longest sequence of dependent tasks that determines the minimum project duration — any delay on the critical path delays the entire project
4. What is scope creep? — **Answer:** Uncontrolled changes or additions to project scope — the number one cause of project failure
5. What tools are used for project scheduling? — **Answer:** Microsoft Project, Asana, Jira, Monday.com, and Smartsheet

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What are the main cost estimation techniques? — **Answer:** Analogous (top-down), parametric, bottom-up, and three-point estimation
2. What is earned value management? — **Answer:** A method that combines scope, schedule, and cost to measure project performance — comparing planned vs actual progress
3. What is CPI (Cost Performance Index)? — **Answer:** A ratio of earned value to actual cost — CPI greater than 1 means under budget; less than 1 means over budget
4. What is a contingency reserve? — **Answer:** A budget set aside for known risks — identified risks that may or may not occur
5. How do you control project costs? — **Answer:** Track actual spending against budget, monitor variances, approve changes through a change control process, and report regularly

4. What is the future of the PM role? — **Answer:** More strategic, more data-driven, more focused on change management and stakeholder engagement — less focused on administrative tasks
5. How do PMs stay relevant in the age of AI? — **Answer:** Develop soft skills (leadership, communication, negotiation), strategic thinking, business acumen, and domain expertise — skills AI cannot replicate
