## YEAR 100 — PM FOUNDATIONS

**Goal:** Produce graduates who can guide products from conception to launch by mastering strategy, execution, user empathy, and data-informed decision-making.

### Semester 1

**PM101 — Foundations of Product Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* The cornerstone of the PM track — introducing the role, product lifecycle, and core mindsets for building successful products.
*Classes:*
1. What is a Product Manager? — Roles, types (B2B, B2C, Growth), key skills
2. The Product Lifecycle — Ideation, Development, Launch, Growth, Maturity, Decline
3. Design Thinking for PMs — Empathy, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test
4. Identifying User Needs — Jobs to be Done framework, pain points vs solutions
5. Market & Competitive Analysis — SWOT, market sizing, Porter's Five Forces
6. Introduction to Metrics — Vanity vs actionable metrics, AARRR framework
7. Product Vision — Defining the "Why", crafting a compelling vision statement
8. Product Strategy — Linking vision to execution, strategic pillars
9. Introduction to Roadmapping — Outcome vs output-based roadmaps
10. Prioritization Frameworks — ICE, RICE, MoSCoW, Kano Model
11. Working with Engineers — Tech debt, effective communication, building trust
12. Working with Designers — Design process, giving feedback, PM/Designer relationship

**DES101 — Introduction to Visual Design** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Foundational visual design principles every PM should understand to collaborate effectively with designers.
*See: [Product Design — DES101](./product-design.md)*

**PM103 — Introduction to Agile & Scrum** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Core Agile and Scrum principles for managing product development in iterative cycles.

**UXR102 — Fundamentals of User Research** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Learn why, when, and how to conduct user research to inform product decisions.

**COM101 — Communication & Stakeholder Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Master the art of communicating with stakeholders, managing expectations, and aligning cross-functional teams.

### Semester 2

**PM102 — Writing User Stories & Requirements** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Learn to write clear, actionable user stories and acceptance criteria that engineers can build from.

**DES104 — Introduction to UX Design** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand UX principles so you can evaluate product experiences and give informed design feedback.

**ANA102 — Fundamentals of Product Analytics** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Learn to define, track, and analyze product metrics to make data-informed decisions.

**DEV102 — HTML & CSS for Product Managers** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand the building blocks of the web to communicate effectively with engineering teams.

**MKT102 — Fundamentals of Product Marketing** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Learn go-to-market fundamentals, positioning, and how to work with marketing teams.

## YEAR 200 — INTERMEDIATE PM CRAFT

### Semester 1

**PM201 — Product Roadmapping & Strategy** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Advanced roadmapping techniques, strategy frameworks, and stakeholder alignment.

**ANA201 — Data-Driven Decision Making** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Statistical analysis, experiment design, and using data to validate product hypotheses.

**UXR201 — Conducting User Interviews & Surveys** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Master techniques for conducting effective user interviews and crafting insightful surveys.

**PM203 — Technical Fundamentals for PMs** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand APIs, databases, system architecture, and technical tradeoffs to make informed product decisions.

**FIN201 — Business & Finance for PMs** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Unit economics, P&L basics, pricing models, and understanding business viability.

### Semester 2

**PM202 — A/B Testing & Experimentation** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Design, run, and analyze A/B tests to validate product changes and optimize conversion.

**PM204 — Growth Hacking & Product-Led Growth** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* PLG strategies, viral loops, activation funnels, and growth experiment frameworks.

**UXR202 — Usability Testing: Planning & Execution** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Plan, conduct, and analyze usability tests to identify and fix product problems.

**DES202 — Interaction Design Principles** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand microinteractions, animations, and delightful UX patterns to evaluate product experiences.

**PM206 — Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Launch planning, channel strategy, messaging, and cross-functional GTM execution.

## YEAR 300 — ADVANCED PM & LEADERSHIP

### Semester 1

**PM301 — Advanced Product Strategy & Portfolio Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Manage multiple products, prioritize across portfolios, and align product strategy with company goals.

**PM303 — Platform Product Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Build and manage platform products, internal tools, APIs, and developer-facing products.

**DES301 — Building & Maintaining Design Systems** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand design systems to scale product development and maintain consistency.

**PM305 — API Product Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Design, launch, and grow API products — authentication, documentation, developer experience.

**LAW301 — Legal & Ethical Considerations in Product** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* GDPR, data privacy, accessibility compliance, and ethical product decisions.

### Semester 2

**PM302 — Product Leadership & Team Building** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Transition from IC PM to product leader — hiring, coaching, and building product teams.

**PM304 — B2B vs B2C Product Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand the differences in user research, sales cycles, and product strategy between B2B and B2C.

**PM306 — Pricing & Monetization Strategies** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Pricing models, packaging, willingness-to-pay research, and monetization experiments.

**ANA302 — Advanced Analytics & Data Science for PMs** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Machine learning basics, predictive analytics, and leveraging data science in product decisions.

**OPS302 — DevOps & Operations for PMs** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Understand deployment pipelines, monitoring, incident response, and operational excellence.

## YEAR 400 — PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE

### Semester 1

**PM401 — Mastering the PM Interview** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Prepare for product management interviews — case studies, product sense, execution, and behavioral rounds.

**PORT401 — Building a Product Portfolio & Case Studies** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Craft compelling product case studies that demonstrate impact, strategy, and execution skills.

**FRE401 — Freelancing & Consulting for PMs** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Build a PM consulting practice — finding clients, pricing, scoping, and delivering value.

**PM403 — AI/ML Product Management** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Manage AI-powered products — model evaluation, data strategy, ethical AI, and ML product lifecycles.

**PM405 — Specialization I** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Choose a domain: FinTech, HealthTech, SaaS, E-commerce, or Social. Deep-dive into domain-specific product challenges.

### Semester 2

**CAP402 — Capstone Project: Build & Launch a Product** *(6 Credits)*
*Description:* Apply everything to a real product — from ideation through launch. Present to industry judges.

**ETH402 — Ethics & Responsibility in Technology** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Explore ethical dilemmas in product development — dark patterns, data privacy, algorithmic bias.

**TEAM402 — Mentorship & Public Speaking** *(3 Credits)*
*Description:* Develop mentorship skills and learn to communicate product vision to large audiences.
