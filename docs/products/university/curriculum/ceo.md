## YEAR 100 — LEADERSHIP FOUNDATIONS

**Goal:** Produce graduates who understand the fundamentals of leadership, business strategy, organizational management, and the role of a CEO in building and scaling companies.

### Semester 1

*Related Articles:*

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is the primary role of a CEO? — **Answer:** To set the organization vision, strategy, and culture — and ensure the organization has the resources and talent to execute
2. What is the difference between a CEO and a founder? — **Answer:** A founder creates the company; a CEO runs it — they can be the same person or different people
3. What are the key CEO responsibilities? — **Answer:** Strategy, team building, capital allocation, culture, stakeholder management, and organizational performance
4. What types of CEOs exist? — **Answer:** Visionary (startup), Operational (scaling), Turnaround (crisis), and Stewardship (mature company)
5. What makes a great CEO? — **Answer:** Vision, decisiveness, emotional intelligence, integrity, adaptability, and the ability to attract and retain top talent

1. What is business strategy? — **Answer:** A plan for how an organization will compete, grow, and create value — defining where to play and how to win
2. What is a value proposition? — **Answer:** A clear statement of the unique value a company delivers to customers — why they should choose you over competitors

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What are the three main financial statements? — **Answer:** Income Statement (P&L), Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement
2. What is the difference between profit and cash flow? — **Answer:** Profit is revenue minus expenses (accounting); cash flow is actual money moving in and out — a company can be profitable but run out of cash
3. What is unit economics? — **Answer:** The direct revenues and costs associated with a single unit of business — e.g., one customer, one product, one transaction
4. What is CAC and why does it matter? — **Answer:** Customer Acquisition Cost — the total cost of acquiring one customer; it must be lower than LTV for a sustainable business
5. What is the most important financial metric for a CEO? — **Answer:** Cash flow — because without cash, the company cannot survive, regardless of profitability

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What are the main organizational structures? — **Answer:** Functional, divisional, matrix, flat, and network structures
2. What is span of control? — **Answer:** The number of direct reports a manager has — typically 5-8 for optimal effectiveness
3. What is a matrix organization? — **Answer:** An structure where employees report to multiple managers — typically a functional manager and a project manager
4. How does organizational structure affect performance? — **Answer:** It determines communication flow, decision-making speed, accountability, and collaboration patterns
5. When should you change organizational structure? — **Answer:** When the current structure no longer supports the strategy, when growth creates complexity, or when performance is declining

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is the OODA loop? — **Answer:** Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — a decision-making framework for rapid, iterative decision making
2. What is analysis paralysis? — **Answer:** Overthinking a decision to the point where no decision is made — a common CEO trap
3. When should a CEO use data vs intuition? — **Answer:** Use data when it is available and reliable; use intuition when data is incomplete, time is critical, or the situation is novel
4. What is a decision framework? — **Answer:** A structured approach to making decisions — defining the problem, gathering information, evaluating options, and choosing
5. How do you improve decision quality? — **Answer:** Gather relevant information, consider multiple perspectives, evaluate tradeoffs, test assumptions, and learn from outcomes
