*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What does AARRR stand for? — **Answer:** Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral — the growth funnel
2. What is the difference between growth marketing and traditional marketing? — **Answer:** Growth marketing is data-driven, full-funnel, and experimental; traditional marketing focuses on top-of-funnel awareness and brand
3. What is a growth team? — **Answer:** A cross-functional team (PM, engineer, designer, marketer, data analyst) focused on moving growth metrics through rapid experimentation
4. What is the North Star Metric? — **Answer:** The single metric that best captures the core value a product delivers to its customers
5. Why is growth especially important in the Nigerian market? — **Answer:** High competition, price-sensitive users, mobile-first behavior, and the need for rapid scale to achieve network effects

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is attribution modeling? — **Answer:** Determining which marketing channels deserve credit for a conversion — first-touch, last-touch, linear, or time-decay
2. What is a cohort? — **Answer:** A group of users who share a common characteristic (e.g., signed up in the same week) — used to track behavior over time
3. What is the difference between vanity metrics and actionable metrics? — **Answer:** Vanity metrics look good but don't drive decisions (total downloads); actionable metrics inform specific decisions (activation rate)
4. What is a growth dashboard? — **Answer:** A visual display of key growth metrics — acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, and referral — updated in real-time
5. Why is cohort analysis important for growth? — **Answer:** It reveals whether product changes are actually improving user behavior over time, not just increasing total numbers

1. What is the Hook Model? — **Answer:** Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment — a framework for building habit-forming products
2. What is loss aversion? — **Answer:** People feel the pain of losing something twice as strongly as the pleasure of gaining something equivalent
3. What is social proof? — **Answer:** The tendency to follow the behavior of others — "if everyone else is doing it, it must be right"
4. What is the scarcity principle? — **Answer:** People value things more when they're limited — "only 3 left" or "offer expires tomorrow"
5. How does understanding psychology help growth? — **Answer:** By designing experiences that align with how humans actually think and behave — not how we wish they would

*Quiz (5 questions):*

2. What is a viral loop? — **Answer:** A self-reinforcing cycle where existing users bring in new users — e.g., "invite a friend and both get a reward"

5. What is the viral coefficient (K-factor)? — **Answer:** The number of new users each existing user brings in — K > 1 means exponential growth
