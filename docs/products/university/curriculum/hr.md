**Goal:** Produce graduates who understand the fundamentals of human resources — recruitment, employee relations, compensation, compliance, and the strategic role of HR in organizations.

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is the primary function of HR? — **Answer:** Managing the employee lifecycle — recruitment, onboarding, development, compensation, and separation
2. What is the difference between HR and People Operations? — **Answer:** HR is traditionally administrative and compliance-focused; People Ops is employee-experience focused and data-driven
3. What are the key HR functions? — **Answer:** Recruitment, training, compensation, employee relations, compliance, performance management, and organizational development
4. Why is HR strategic? — **Answer:** Because people are the most important asset — HR aligns people strategy with business strategy
5. What is the role of HR in Nigerian organizations? — **Answer:** Talent acquisition, compliance with labor laws, employee development, compensation management, and organizational culture

1. What is a salary structure? — **Answer:** A framework that defines pay ranges for different job levels — ensuring internal equity and external competitiveness
2. What is total rewards? — **Answer:** The complete package of compensation, benefits, recognition, development, and work environment that an organization offers
3. What are common employee benefits in Nigeria? — **Answer:** Health insurance, pension (contributory pension scheme), leave, bonuses, and transportation allowance

5. Why is compensation benchmarking important? — **Answer:** To ensure salaries are competitive with the market — preventing underpayment (turnover) and overpayment (cost inefficiency)

1. What is the goal of onboarding? — **Answer:** To help new employees become productive, engaged, and integrated members of the organization as quickly as possible
2. How long should onboarding last? — **Answer:** At least 90 days — with structured activities at 30, 60, and 90-day milestones
3. What is an exit interview? — **Answer:** A conversation with a departing employee to understand their reasons for leaving and gather feedback for improvement
4. What is knowledge transfer? — **Answer:** The process of capturing and sharing a departing employee knowledge, skills, and institutional memory
5. Why is offboarding important? — **Answer:** It protects institutional knowledge, maintains brand reputation, ensures legal compliance, and can turn departing employees into brand advocates
