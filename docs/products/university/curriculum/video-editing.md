*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is video editing? — **Answer:** The process of selecting, arranging, and assembling raw footage into a coherent, engaging final product
2. What is the difference between a codec and a container? — **Answer:** A codec compresses/decompresses video data; a container (MP4, MOV) holds the video, audio, and metadata together
3. What is the editing workflow? — **Answer:** Import → organize → rough cut → fine cut → color grade → audio mix → export
4. What is the "language of film"? — **Answer:** The visual grammar of filmmaking — shots, angles, transitions, pacing, and how they communicate meaning
5. What is the editor's most important skill? — **Answer:** Storytelling — knowing what to keep, what to cut, and how to arrange footage for maximum emotional impact

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. What is Walter Murch's Rule of Six? — **Answer:** The six criteria for a good cut: emotion (51%), story (23%), rhythm (10%), eye-trace (7%), 2D plane (5%), 3D space (4%)
2. What is a jump cut? — **Answer:** A cut between two similar shots that creates a jarring, discontinuous effect — often used intentionally for stylistic purposes
3. What is pacing in editing? — **Answer:** The speed and rhythm of cuts — fast pacing creates energy and tension; slow pacing creates mood and reflection
4. What is an invisible edit? — **Answer:** A cut so seamless the audience doesn't notice it — the story flows naturally without distraction
5. What is the most important criterion for deciding when to cut? — **Answer:** Emotion — cut when the emotional beat of the scene has been delivered

*Quiz (5 questions):*
1. Why is audio more important than video quality? — **Answer:** Viewers will tolerate poor video but not poor audio — bad audio causes immediate viewer drop-off
2. What is dialogue cleanup? — **Answer:** Removing background noise, hum, and unwanted sounds from recorded dialogue
3. What is the ideal audio level for dialogue? — **Answer:** -6dB to -12dB — loud enough to be clear but not clipping
4. What is the difference between diegetic and non-diegetic sound? — **Answer:** Diegetic: sound that exists in the story world (characters can hear it); non-diegetic: sound only the audience hears (background music)
5. Where can you find royalty-free music? — **Answer:** Epidemic Sound, Artlist, YouTube Audio Library, and FreeMusicArchive

2. What is the 3-2-1 backup rule? — **Answer:** 3 copies of your data, on 2 different types of media, with 1 copy stored offsite

5. What is the biggest mistake in file management? — **Answer:** Storing everything on one drive with no backup — a single drive failure can destroy an entire project
