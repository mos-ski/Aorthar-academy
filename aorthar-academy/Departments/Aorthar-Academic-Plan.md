# Aorthar Academy: Faculty of Product Development

This document outlines the academic plan, course structure, and lesson frameworks for the departments within the Faculty of Product Development.

## Faculty-Wide Foundation

All students, regardless of department, are part of the Faculty of Product Development. In Year 100, Semester 1, all students share a set of foundational courses to ensure a common language and understanding of the product lifecycle.

**Shared First Semester Courses:**
- **PM101**: Foundations of Product Management
- **DES101**: Introduction to Visual Design
- **PM103**: Introduction to Agile & Scrum

### **Course Deep Dive: PM101 - Foundations of Product Management**

**Description**: This course is the cornerstone of the Product Management track, introducing students to the role of a PM, the product lifecycle, and the core mindsets required to build successful products.

**12-Week Lesson Plan:**

| Week | Topic | Core Concepts | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | What is a Product Manager? | The role & responsibilities, types of PMs (B2B, B2C, Growth), key skills. | [What is Product Management?](https://www.productplan.com/what-is-product-management/), [The Product Manager Role](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=502ILHjX9EE) |
| 2 | The Product Lifecycle | Ideation, Development, Launch, Growth, Maturity, Decline. | [The Product Life Cycle](https://www.productplan.com/learn/product-lifecycle/), [Product Lifecycle Stages](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ksyA-c6d55A) |
| 3 | Design Thinking for PMs | Empathy, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test. Applying the framework to find problems. | [IDEO's Design Thinking](https://designthinking.ideo.com/), [Design Thinking in 5 Steps](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZfU5Y7F3Vwk) |
| 4 | Identifying User Needs | "Jobs to be Done" framework, identifying pain points vs. solutions. | [Jobs To Be Done Explained](https://jtbd.info/), [Clay Christensen on JTBD](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfGtw2C4R_g) |
| 5 | Market & Competitive Analysis | SWOT analysis, identifying direct/indirect competitors, market sizing. | [How to Run a Competitive Analysis](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/competitive-analysis-kit), [Porter's Five Forces](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mYF2_FBCvXw) |
| 6 | Introduction to Metrics | Vanity vs. Actionable metrics, AARRR framework (Pirate Metrics). | [AARRR Framework](https://www.productled.org/foundations/aarrr-framework), [The only metric that matters](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yA91VwGsoO4) |
| 7 | What is a Product Vision? | Defining the "Why", crafting a compelling vision statement. | [Product Vision by Marty Cagan](https://www.svpg.com/product-vision/), [How to Write a Product Vision](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mN3E_3sLg2c) |
| 8 | What is a Product Strategy? | Linking vision to execution, strategic pillars, defining your moat. | [What is Product Strategy?](https://www.productboard.com/blog/what-is-product-strategy/), [Good Strategy / Bad Strategy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1-gTidy7n68) |
| 9 | Introduction to Roadmapping | What a roadmap is (and isn't), outcome vs. output-based roadmaps. | [Guide to Product Roadmaps](https://www.atlassian.com/roadmunk/guide/product-roadmaps), [The Problem with Roadmaps](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v12YvGk2GkM) |
| 10 | Prioritization Frameworks | ICE, RICE, MoSCoW, Kano Model. How to say "no". | [20 Product Prioritization Frameworks](https://www.productboard.com/blog/20-product-prioritization-frameworks/), [The Art of Saying No](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VpUW16s3a-w) |
| 11 | Working with Engineers | Understanding tech debt, effective communication, building trust. | [How to Work with Engineers](https://www.bringwith.com/blog/how-to-work-with-engineers-as-a-pm), [A PM's Guide to Tech Debt](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gG2n_2YSQLI) |
| 12 | Working with Designers | The design process, giving feedback, the PM/Designer relationship. | [How PMs can work with Designers](https://uxdesign.cc/how-pms-can-work-with-designers-1f0c27b637e7), [Design for Product People](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zL2Fp_f4dgo) |

**Sample Quiz Questions (Weeks 1-4):**

1.  **Which of the following is NOT a primary responsibility of a Product Manager?**
    a) Defining the product vision
    b) Writing the code for new features
    c) Prioritizing features for development
    d) Understanding user needs

2.  **In the Product Lifecycle, what stage typically follows "Launch"?**
    a) Decline
    b) Ideation
    c) Growth
    d) Maturity

3.  **What is the first step in the Design Thinking process?**
    a) Prototype
    b) Ideate
    c) Define
    d) Empathize

4.  **The "Jobs to be Done" framework focuses on:**
    a) The demographic of the user
    b) The features a product has
    c) The progress a user is trying to make
    d) The user's past purchase history

**Final Exam Structure:**
- **Part A: Multiple Choice (40%)**: 40 questions covering key definitions and concepts from all 12 weeks.
- **Part B: Short Answer (30%)**: 3 questions requiring 2-3 paragraph answers.
  - *Example*: "Compare and contrast the RICE and MoSCoW prioritization frameworks. In what scenario would you choose one over the other?"
- **Part C: Scenario-Based Essay (30%)**: 1 long-form question based on a hypothetical product scenario.
  - *Example*: "You are the new PM for a struggling food delivery app. User growth is flat and customer complaints about late deliveries are high. Using concepts from the product lifecycle, user needs, and strategy, outline your first 90-day plan."
