### **Course Deep Dive: SCR101 - Agile Principles & Scrum Mastery**

**Description**: This course is the entry point for aspiring Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches. It covers the history of Agile, the Scrum framework, and the servant-leadership mindset required to support high-performing teams.

**12-Week Lesson Plan:**

| Week | Topic | Core Concepts | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Waterfall vs. Agile | The history of software development, limitations of Waterfall, the rise of Agile. | [Waterfall vs Agile](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=502ILHjX9EE) |
| 2 | The Agile Manifesto | The 4 Values and 12 Principles of Agile software development. | [Agile Manifesto](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9QbYZh1YXY) |
| 3 | Scrum Framework Overview | Empiricism (Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation), Scrum Values. | [Scrum Framework](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9TycLR0TqFA) |
| 4 | Scrum Roles: The Scrum Master | Servant leadership, removing impediments, coaching the team. | [Scrum Master Role](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yo5sK-99e8U) |
| 5 | Scrum Roles: PO & Developers | The Product Owner's accountability vs. the Developers' accountability. | [Scrum Roles](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8v1rL5M8bI) |
| 6 | Scrum Artifacts: The Backlog | Product Backlog vs. Sprint Backlog, Definition of Done (DoD). | [Product Backlog](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1-gTidy7n68) |
| 7 | Scrum Artifacts: The Increment | What constitutes a shippable increment, technical debt. | [Increment](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7rQmVq9f7v4) |
| 8 | Scrum Events: Sprint Planning | How to facilitate planning, estimation (Story Points/Poker), Sprint Goal. | [Sprint Planning](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vb4V4n3mRUE) |
| 9 | Scrum Events: Daily Scrum | Purpose of the standup, avoiding status updates, the 3 questions. | [Daily Scrum](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Jj6e6lO7n8) |
| 10 | Scrum Events: Sprint Review | Inspecting the increment, stakeholder feedback, adapting the backlog. | [Sprint Review](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XIn4-XfJqH0) |
| 11 | Scrum Events: Sprint Retrospective | Continuous improvement, facilitation techniques (Start/Stop/Continue). | [Sprint Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2p9sIugbIwo) |
| 12 | Kanban & Scrumban | Visualizing work, WIP limits, flow efficiency, differences from Scrum. | [Kanban vs Scrum](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9QbYZh1YXY) |

**Sample Quiz Questions (Weeks 1-4):**

1.  **Which of the following is NOT a value in the Agile Manifesto?**
    a) Individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
    b) Working software over comprehensive documentation.
    c) Contract negotiation over customer collaboration.
    d) Responding to change over following a plan.

2.  **What are the three pillars of Scrum (Empiricism)?**
    a) Planning, Execution, Delivery.
    b) Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation.
    c) Respect, Courage, Openness.
    d) Roles, Events, Artifacts.

3.  **Who is responsible for maximizing the value of the product?**
    a) The Scrum Master.
    b) The Developers.
    c) The Product Owner.
    d) The CEO.

4.  **The Scrum Master is best described as a:**
    a) Project Manager.
    b) Servant Leader.
    c) Team Boss.
    d) Product Designer.

**Final Exam Structure:**
- **Part A: Multiple Choice (40%)**: 40 questions on the Scrum Guide, Agile Manifesto, and roles.
- **Part B: Situational Judgment (30%)**: 5 scenarios where a Scrum Master faces a problem. The student must choose the best course of action.
  - *Example*: "The Product Owner keeps adding items to the Sprint Backlog in the middle of the Sprint, endangering the Sprint Goal. As a Scrum Master, what do you do?"
- **Part C: Facilitation Plan (30%)**: Design a 1-hour Sprint Retrospective agenda for a team that has low morale and missed their last deadline. Include specific activities and timeboxes.
