### **Course Deep Dive: DES101 - Introduction to Visual Design**

**Description**: This course establishes the foundational elements and principles of visual design. Students will learn the universal rules that govern composition, hierarchy, and aesthetics, forming the bedrock for all future UI and graphic design work.

**12-Week Lesson Plan:**


| Week | Topic                                            | Core Concepts                                                                   | Resources                                                                          |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | The Elements of Design: Line & Shape             | Understanding how lines and shapes form the basis of all visuals.               | [The 7 Elements of Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5kvop_A4Q2I)            |
| 2    | The Elements of Design: Form, Texture, & Space   | Adding depth and feel to designs; understanding positive/negative space.        | [Space in Graphic Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hD-4eoxO0Lw)             |
| 3    | The Principles of Design: Balance & Proximity    | Creating stability and visual grouping in a composition.                        | [The Principles of Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5I2C2f1L3Yk)            |
| 4    | The Principles of Design: Alignment & Repetition | Creating order and consistency in a design.                                     | [Alignment, Repetition, and Contrast](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xWpW_e6a4Xw) |
| 5    | The Principles of Design: Contrast & White Space | Creating emphasis and giving a design room to breathe.                          | [Using Contrast and Scale](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5I2C2f1L3Yk&t=245s)     |
| 6    | Introduction to Color Theory                     | The color wheel, primary/secondary/tertiary colors, and basic harmonies.        | [Color Theory In Brief](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AvgCkHrcj90)               |
| 7    | Color Psychology & Application                   | How colors evoke emotion and how to build a color palette.                      | [How To Choose Colors](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fYG6kL2p3c8)                |
| 8    | Introduction to Typography                       | The anatomy of type, serif vs. sans-serif, and the role of fonts.               | [A Crash Course in Typography](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gDbKxTCaJpE)        |
| 9    | Typographic Hierarchy & Pairing                  | Using size, weight, and font choices to guide the user's eye.                   | [How to Choose & Pair Fonts](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsl-w3I_13s)          |
| 10   | Introduction to Grids & Layout                   | The purpose of grids; understanding columns, gutters, and margins.              | [A Guide to Grid Systems](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4vP5K9I5V9s)             |
| 11   | Composition & Visual Hierarchy                   | Putting it all together to direct attention to what's most important.           | [Visual Hierarchy in UX/UI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O38Id_3-c9E)           |
| 12   | Applying Principles to a Simple UI               | A practical exercise in applying all learned concepts to a basic screen design. | [Design a Simple UI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c9Wg6Cb_YlU)                  |


**Sample Quiz Questions (Weeks 1-4):**

1. **Which of the following is considered an "Element" of design?**
  a) Balance
    b) Contrast
    c) Shape
    d) Repetition
2. **The empty area around objects in a design is commonly referred to as:**
  a) Positive Space
    b) Negative Space / White Space
    c) Texture
    d) Form
3. **Placing related items close together in a design is an example of which principle?**
  a) Proximity
    b) Alignment
    c) Contrast
    d) Balance
4. **Creating a structured and organized look by lining up elements is known as:**
  a) Repetition
    b) Alignment
    c) Proximity
    d) Space

**Final Exam Structure:**

- **Part A: Multiple Choice (50%)**: 50 questions covering key definitions and concepts from all 12 weeks (e.g., "What is a serif?").
- **Part B: Visual Analysis (50%)**: 5 questions showing different UI screenshots. Students must write a short analysis for each, identifying which design principles are used effectively or ineffectively.
  - *Example*: "Analyze the use of contrast and hierarchy in the provided image. Is the main call-to-action clear? Why or why not?"

### **Course Deep Dive: DES102 - UI Design Fundamentals**

**Description**: This course moves from the theory of visual design to the practical application of UI design. Students will learn to design and build common user interface components, understand layout systems in practice, and create interfaces that are both functional and easy to use.

**12-Week Lesson Plan:**

| Week | Topic | Core Concepts | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Role of UI vs. UX | Clarifying the relationship and responsibilities of UI and UX designers. | [UI vs. UX Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c9L3s3gWp1U) |
| 2 | Layout & Composition in Practice | Applying grids, the 8pt grid system, creating balanced layouts. | [The 8pt Grid System in UI Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zW9dYqvF0k) |
| 3 | Designing Common Components: Buttons | Anatomy of a button, states (default, hover, disabled), primary vs. secondary. | [Button Design UX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O38Id_3-c9E&t=300s) |
| 4 | Designing Common Components: Forms & Inputs | Input fields, labels, placeholders, validation states, and best practices. | [Forms in UI Design](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G3e-cpL7ofc&t=180s) |
| 5 | Designing Common Components: Cards & Modals | Structuring content with cards, use cases for modals and dialogs. | [Card Design Best Practices](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PAjI-VnNBL8&t=240s) |
| 6 | Navigation Design | Designing tabs, sidebars, breadcrumbs, and other navigation patterns. | [Navigation Design Patterns](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bMknfKXIFA8&t=420s) |
| 7 | Designing for Different States | Empty states, loading states, error states, and success states. | [How to Design Empty States](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c9Wg6Cb_YlU&t=600s) |
| 8 | Introduction to Responsive Design | Mobile-first vs. desktop-first, understanding breakpoints. | [Responsive Design Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v1e1-N0_wlg) |
| 9 | Iconography & Imagery | Choosing and using icons, sourcing images, accessibility for visuals. | [A Guide to Iconography](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sByzHoiYFX0&t=480s) |
| 10 | Light & Dark Mode | Principles of designing for both light and dark user interfaces. | [Designing Dark Mode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fYG6kL2p3c8&t=360s) |
| 11 | Building a Small UI Kit | Creating reusable components (buttons, inputs) in Figma. | [Build a UI Kit in Figma](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p6gNTC_a0k8) |
| 12 | Design Handoff | How to prepare and document designs for developers. | [Design Handoff Process](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3q3FV65-xLg&t=720s) |

**Sample Quiz Questions (Weeks 1-4):**

1.  **What is the primary goal of UI design?**
    a) To conduct user research and create personas.
    b) To facilitate a user's interaction with a product and guide them through it.
    c) To write the code that makes the product work.
    d) To define the product's business strategy.

2.  **The 8pt grid system is used to ensure:**
    a) The color palette is accessible.
    b) The code is free of bugs.
    c) Spacing and sizing of elements are consistent and scalable.
    d) The marketing campaign is effective.

3.  **The "hover" state of a button indicates:**
    a) The button has been permanently disabled.
    b) The user's cursor is currently over the button.
    c) The button has been successfully clicked.
    d) The button is the most important action on the page.

4.  **In a form, what is the purpose of a placeholder text?**
    a) To provide the final user input.
    b) To act as a permanent label for the input field.
    c) To give a hint or example of the required input.
    d) To validate the user's data.

**Final Exam Structure:**
- **Part A: Multiple Choice (40%)**: 40 questions on UI components, states, and best practices.
- **Part B: UI Critique (30%)**: Students are shown a screenshot of a poorly designed mobile app screen. They must identify 3-5 specific UI issues and explain how they would fix them, referencing principles learned in the course.
- **Part C: Practical Design Task (30%)**: "You are tasked with designing a 'Create Account' screen for a new music streaming app. Sketch or wireframe the screen, including all necessary input fields, buttons, and links. Justify your layout choices."
